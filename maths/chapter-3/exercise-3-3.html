Elimination Method — How it works:

  • Step 1: Multiply one or both equations to make coefficients of one variable equal.
  • Step 2: Add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable.
  • Step 3: Solve the resulting equation to get one variable.
  • Step 4: Substitute back to get the other variable.
  • Step 5: Verify both values in original equations.

Question 1

Question:

Solve the following pair of linear equations by the elimination method and the substitution method:

(i) x + y = 5 and 2x – 3y = 4

(ii) 3x + 4y = 10 and 2x – 2y = 2

(iii) 3x – 5y – 4 = 0 and 9x = 2y + 7

(iv) x/2 + 2y/3 = –1 and x – y/3 = 3

Solution (i): x + y = 5 and 2x – 3y = 4

By Elimination Method:

x + y = 5   ... (1)

2x – 3y = 4   ... (2)

Step 1: Multiply equation (1) by 3:

3x + 3y = 15   ... (3)

Step 2: Add equation (2) and (3):

3x + 3y = 15

2x – 3y = 4

————————

5x = 19

x = 19/5

Step 3: Put x = 19/5 in equation (1):

19/5 + y = 5

y = 5 – 19/5 = 25/5 – 19/5 = 6/5

Verify: x + y = 19/5 + 6/5 = 25/5 = 5 ✅

2(19/5) – 3(6/5) = 38/5 – 18/5 = 20/5 = 4 ✅

Answer: x = 19/5, y = 6/5

Solution (ii): 3x + 4y = 10 and 2x – 2y = 2

By Elimination Method:

3x + 4y = 10   ... (1)

2x – 2y = 2 → x – y = 1   ... (2)

Step 1: Multiply equation (2) by 4:

4x – 4y = 4   ... (3)

Step 2: Add equation (1) and (3):

3x + 4y = 10

4x – 4y = 4

————————

7x = 14

x = 2

Step 3: Put x = 2 in equation (2):

2 – y = 1 → y = 1

Verify: 3(2) + 4(1) = 6 + 4 = 10 ✅

2(2) – 2(1) = 4 – 2 = 2 ✅

Answer: x = 2, y = 1

Solution (iii): 3x – 5y – 4 = 0 and 9x = 2y + 7

Rewrite in standard form:

3x – 5y = 4   ... (1)

9x – 2y = 7   ... (2)

Step 1: Multiply equation (1) by 3:

9x – 15y = 12   ... (3)

Step 2: Subtract equation (2) from (3):

9x – 15y = 12

9x – 2y = 7

————————

–13y = 5

y = –5/13

Step 3: Put y = –5/13 in equation (1):

3x – 5(–5/13) = 4

3x + 25/13 = 4

3x = 4 – 25/13 = 52/13 – 25/13 = 27/13

x = 9/13

Verify: 3(9/13) – 5(–5/13) = 27/13 + 25/13 = 52/13 = 4 ✅

Answer: x = 9/13, y = –5/13

Solution (iv): x/2 + 2y/3 = –1 and x – y/3 = 3

Step 1: Multiply equation (1) by 6:

3x + 4y = –6   ... (1)

Step 2: Multiply equation (2) by 3:

3x – y = 9   ... (2)

Step 3: Subtract equation (2) from (1):

3x + 4y = –6

3x – y = 9

————————

5y = –15

y = –3

Step 4: Put y = –3 in equation (2):

3x – (–3) = 9

3x + 3 = 9

3x = 6 → x = 2

Verify: 2/2 + 2(–3)/3 = 1 – 2 = –1 ✅

2 – (–3)/3 = 2 + 1 = 3 ✅

Answer: x = 2, y = –3

Question 2

Question:

Form the pair of linear equations in the following problems, and find their solutions (if they exist) by the elimination method:

(i) If we add 1 to the numerator and subtract 1 from the denominator, a fraction reduces to 1. It becomes 1/2 if we only add 1 to the denominator. What is the fraction?

(ii) Five years ago, Nuri was thrice as old as Sonu. Ten years later, Nuri will be twice as old as Sonu. How old are Nuri and Sonu?

(iii) The sum of the digits of a two-digit number is 9. Also, nine times this number is twice the number obtained by reversing the order of the digits. Find the number.

(iv) Meena went to a bank to withdraw Rs 2000. She asked the cashier to give her Rs 50 and Rs 100 notes only. Meena got 25 notes in all. Find how many notes of Rs 50 and Rs 100 she received.

(v) A lending library has a fixed charge for the first three days and an additional charge for each day thereafter. Saritha paid Rs 27 for a book kept for seven days, while Susy paid Rs 21 for the book she kept for five days. Find the fixed charge and the charge for each extra day.

Solution (i): Fraction problem

Let fraction = x/y

(x+1)/(y–1) = 1 → x + 1 = y – 1 → x – y = –2 ... (1)

x/(y+1) = 1/2 → 2x = y + 1 → 2x – y = 1 ... (2)

Subtract (1) from (2):

2x – y = 1

x – y = –2

————————

x = 3

Put x = 3 in (1): 3 – y = –2 → y = 5

Answer: The fraction is 3/5.

Solution (ii): Nuri and Sonu age problem

Let Nuri's age = x, Sonu's age = y

Five years ago: x – 5 = 3(y – 5) → x – 3y = –10 ... (1)

Ten years later: x + 10 = 2(y + 10) → x – 2y = 10 ... (2)

Subtract (1) from (2):

x – 2y = 10

x – 3y = –10

————————

y = 20

Put y = 20 in (2): x – 40 = 10 → x = 50

Answer: Nuri's age = 50 years, Sonu's age = 20 years.

Solution (iii): Two digit number, sum of digits = 9

Let tens digit = x, units digit = y

Number = 10x + y, Reversed = 10y + x

x + y = 9 ... (1)

9(10x + y) = 2(10y + x)

90x + 9y = 20y + 2x

88x – 11y = 0 → 8x – y = 0 ... (2)

Add (1) and (2):

x + y = 9

8x – y = 0

————————

9x = 9 → x = 1

Put x = 1 in (1): y = 8

Answer: The number is 18.

Solution (iv): Meena's notes problem

Let number of Rs 50 notes = x, Rs 100 notes = y

x + y = 25 ... (1)

50x + 100y = 2000 → x + 2y = 40 ... (2)

Subtract (1) from (2):

x + 2y = 40

x + y = 25

————————

y = 15

Put y = 15 in (1): x = 10

Answer: Rs 50 notes = 10, Rs 100 notes = 15.

Solution (v): Library fixed charge problem

Let fixed charge = Rs x, extra charge per day = Rs y

Saritha: x + 4y = 27 ... (1) (7–3 = 4 extra days)

Susy: x + 2y = 21 ... (2) (5–3 = 2 extra days)

Subtract (2) from (1):

x + 4y = 27

x + 2y = 21

————————

2y = 6 → y = 3

Put y = 3 in (2): x + 6 = 21 → x = 15

Answer: Fixed charge = Rs 15, Extra charge per day = Rs 3.

Key Points – Exercise 3.3

  • Elimination method is best when coefficients are easy to match.
  • Multiply equations to make coefficients of one variable equal.
  • Add equations if signs are opposite, subtract if signs are same.
  • Always verify answers in both original equations.
  • Remove fractions first by multiplying with LCM.
