Class 10 Maths Chapter 3 Exercise 3.1 Solutions 2026-27 English Medium

These are the NCERT Solutions for Class 10 Maths Chapter 3 – Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables, Exercise 3.1 as per the 2026-27 syllabus.

How to solve graphically:

  • Find at least 2 points for each equation by putting x = 0 and y = 0
  • Plot both lines on the graph
  • Point of intersection = solution
  • Parallel lines = no solution (inconsistent)
  • Same line = infinite solutions

Question 1

Question:

Aftab tells his daughter, "Seven years ago, I was seven times as old as you were then. Also, three years from now, I shall be three times as old as you will be." Represent this situation algebraically and graphically.

Solution:

Step 1: Form equations.

Let present age of Aftab = x years

Let present age of daughter = y years

Seven years ago: Aftab's age = x–7, Daughter's age = y–7

Condition: x–7 = 7(y–7)

x–7 = 7y–49

x – 7y + 42 = 0 ... (1)

Three years from now: Aftab's age = x+3, Daughter's age = y+3

Condition: x+3 = 3(y+3)

x+3 = 3y+9

x – 3y – 6 = 0 ... (2)

Step 2: Make tables of values.

For equation (1): x = 7y – 42

When y = 6 → x = 42–42 = 0 → Point (0, 6)

When y = 8 → x = 56–42 = 14 → Point (14, 8)

When y = 10 → x = 70–42 = 28 → Point (28, 10)

For equation (2): x = 3y + 6

When y = 0 → x = 6 → Point (6, 0)

When y = 6 → x = 24 → Point (24, 6)

When y = 8 → x = 30 → Point (30, 8)

Step 3: Plot the graph.

Step 4: Find intersection.

From equation (1) and (2):

x – 7y + 42 = 0 ... (1)

x – 3y – 6 = 0   ... (2)

Subtract (2) from (1): –4y + 48 = 0 → y = 12

Put y = 12 in (2): x = 3(12) + 6 = 42

Answer: Present age of Aftab = 42 years, Daughter = 12 years.

The two lines intersect at point (42, 12).

Question 2

Question:

The coach of a cricket team buys 3 bats and 6 balls for Rs 3900. Later, she buys another bat and 3 more balls of the same kind for Rs 1300. Represent this situation algebraically and geometrically.

Solution:

Step 1: Form equations.

Let cost of one bat = Rs x

Let cost of one ball = Rs y

Condition 1: 3x + 6y = 3900 → x + 2y = 1300 ... (1)

Condition 2: x + 3y = 1300 ... (2)

Step 2: Table of values.

For equation (1): x = 1300 – 2y

y = 0 → x = 1300 → (1300, 0)

y = 300 → x = 700 → (700, 300)

y = 650 → x = 0 → (0, 650)

For equation (2): x = 1300 – 3y

y = 0 → x = 1300 → (1300, 0)

y = 300 → x = 400 → (400, 300)

y = 433 → x = 0 → (0, 433)

Step 3: Plot the graph.

Answer: The two lines meet at (1300, 0). This means cost of bat = Rs 1300 and ball = Rs 0, which is not realistic. The equations represent the situation algebraically and geometrically as required.

Question 3

Question:

The cost of 2 kg of apples and 1 kg of grapes on a particular day was found to be Rs 160. After a month, the cost of 4 kg of apples and 2 kg of grapes is Rs 300. Represent the situation algebraically and geometrically.

Solution:

Step 1: Form equations.

Let cost of 1 kg apples = Rs x

Let cost of 1 kg grapes = Rs y

Condition 1: 2x + y = 160 ... (1)

Condition 2: 4x + 2y = 300 → 2x + y = 150 ... (2)

Step 2: Table of values.

For equation (1): y = 160 – 2x

x = 0 → y = 160 → (0, 160)

x = 40 → y = 80 → (40, 80)

x = 80 → y = 0 → (80, 0)

For equation (2): y = 150 – 2x

x = 0 → y = 150 → (0, 150)

x = 40 → y = 70 → (40, 70)

x = 75 → y = 0 → (75, 0)

Step 3: Plot the graph.

Step 4: Check type of solution.

Equation (1): 2x + y = 160

Equation (2): 2x + y = 150

Here a₁/a₂ = 2/2 = 1, b₁/b₂ = 1/1 = 1, c₁/c₂ = 160/150 ≠ 1

Since a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ ≠ c₁/c₂ → Lines are parallel. No solution.

Answer: The two lines are parallel and never intersect. The situation is inconsistent — prices cannot satisfy both conditions.

Key Points – Exercise 3.1

  • Always find at least 3 points for each line before plotting.
  • Intersecting lines → unique solution (consistent).
  • Parallel lines → no solution (inconsistent).
  • Same/coincident lines → infinite solutions.
  • Graphical method gives approximate answers — use algebraic method for exact values.
