Key Theorems – Chapter 6 Triangles

Basic Proportionality Theorem (BPT): If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle, it divides the other two sides in the same ratio.

If DE ∥ BC, then AD⁄DB = AE⁄EC

Criteria for Similarity:

  • AA (Angle-Angle): Two angles of one triangle equal two angles of another.
  • SSS (Side-Side-Side): All three sides are in proportion.
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side): Two sides in proportion and included angle equal.

Area of Similar Triangles:

ar(△ABC)⁄ar(△PQR) = (AB⁄PQ)² = (BC⁄QR)² = (AC⁄PR)²

Pythagoras Theorem: In a right triangle, square of hypotenuse = sum of squares of other two sides.

AC² = AB² + BC²

Converse of Pythagoras Theorem: If AC² = AB² + BC², then angle B = 90°.

Important Points to Remember

  • Similar figures have same shape but different size.
  • Congruent figures have same shape AND same size.
  • All circles are similar. All squares are similar.
  • Corresponding angles of similar triangles are equal.
  • Corresponding sides of similar triangles are in proportion.
  • Ratio of areas = square of ratio of corresponding sides.
