Key Concepts:

  • Similar figures: Same shape but different size. All corresponding angles are equal and corresponding sides are in proportion.
  • Congruent figures: Same shape and same size. Every pair of congruent figures is similar, but similar figures need not be congruent.
  • Condition for similarity of polygons: Two polygons with the same number of sides are similar only if both conditions hold together, corresponding angles equal and corresponding sides proportional. Either condition alone is not enough.

Question 1

Fill in the blanks using the correct word given in the brackets:

(i) All circles are __________. (congruent, similar)

Solution:

All circles have the same shape but can have different sizes (different radii).

Answer: All circles are similar.

(ii) All squares are __________. (similar, congruent)

Solution:

All squares have the same shape (all angles = 90 degrees) but can have different sizes.

Answer: All squares are similar.

(iii) All __________ triangles are similar. (isosceles, equilateral)

Solution:

In an equilateral triangle, every angle is always 60 degrees, so any two equilateral triangles automatically have equal corresponding angles and proportional sides. An isosceles triangle can have any angle at its apex, so two isosceles triangles need not have the same shape.

Answer: All equilateral triangles are similar.

(iv) Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar, if (a) their corresponding angles are __________ and (b) their corresponding sides are __________. (equal, proportional)

Solution:

Similarity of polygons requires both conditions to hold at the same time, matching angles alone (as in a rectangle and a square) or matching side ratios alone is not sufficient by itself.

Answer: (a) equal (b) proportional.

Question 2

Give two different examples of pair of

(i) similar figures.

(ii) non-similar figures.

Solution:

(i) Similar figures:

Example 1: Any two circles are similar figures, whatever be their radii, since they always have the same shape.

Example 2: Any two squares are similar figures, whatever be the length of their sides, since all angles are 90 degrees in both and all sides are in the same ratio.

(ii) Non-similar figures:

Example 1: A circle and a square are non-similar figures, since a circle has no straight sides while a square has four straight sides.

Example 2: An equilateral triangle and a scalene triangle are non-similar figures, since their corresponding angles are not equal.

Question 3

State whether the following quadrilaterals are similar or not:

Solution:

Quadrilateral PQRS has all four sides equal to 1.5 cm, so it is a rhombus, but its angles are not equal to 90 degrees.

Quadrilateral ABCD has all four sides equal to 3 cm and all angles equal to 90 degrees, so it is a square.

The ratio of every corresponding side is the same, namely 1.5 3 which equals 1 by 2, but the corresponding angles of PQRS are not equal to the corresponding angles of ABCD.

Answer: Since the corresponding angles are not equal, PQRS and ABCD are not similar.
