Class 10 Maths Chapter 2 Exercise 2.1 Solutions 2026-27 English Medium

Key Concept: The zeroes of a polynomial p(x) are the x-coordinates of the points where the graph of y = p(x) crosses or touches the x-axis.

  • Graph crosses x-axis at 2 points → 2 zeroes
  • Graph touches x-axis at 1 point → 1 zero (repeated)
  • Graph does not touch x-axis → 0 zeroes
  • A polynomial of degree n has at most n zeroes

Question 1

Question:

The graphs of y = p(x) are given below for some polynomials p(x). Find the number of zeroes of p(x) in each case.

Part (i)

Step 1: The graph is a straight horizontal line above the x-axis.

Step 2: The graph does not touch or cross the x-axis at any point.

Answer: Number of zeroes = 0

Part (ii)

Step 1: The graph is a straight line (linear polynomial).

Step 2: The graph crosses the x-axis at exactly 1 point.

Answer: Number of zeroes = 1

Part (iii)

Step 1: Look at the graph of y = p(x).

Step 2: The graph crosses the x-axis at 3 points (z1, z2, z3).

Answer: Number of zeroes = 3

Part (iv)

Step 1: The graph is a parabola (quadratic polynomial).

Step 2: The graph crosses the x-axis at exactly 2 points.

Answer: Number of zeroes = 2

Part (v)

Step 1: Look at the graph — it has 4 intersection points with the x-axis.

Step 2: The graph crosses the x-axis at exactly 4 points.

Answer: Number of zeroes = 4

Part (vi)

Step 1: Look at the graph — it is a cubic polynomial curve.

Step 2: The graph crosses the x-axis at exactly 3 points.

Answer: Number of zeroes = 3

Summary of All Answers

(i) 0 zeroes — graph does not touch x-axis

(ii) 1 zero — graph crosses x-axis at 1 point

(iii) 3 zeroes — graph crosses x-axis at 3 points

(iv) 2 zeroes — graph crosses x-axis at 2 points

(v) 4 zeroes — graph crosses x-axis at 4 points

(vi) 3 zeroes — graph crosses x-axis at 3 points

Key Points – Exercise 2.1

  • Zero of polynomial = x-coordinate where graph meets x-axis.
  • Number of zeroes = Number of times graph crosses or touches x-axis.
  • Linear polynomial → exactly 1 zero.
  • Quadratic polynomial → at most 2 zeroes → parabola shape.
  • Cubic polynomial → at most 3 zeroes.
  • If graph only touches x-axis but does not cross → still a zero (repeated zero).
