Exercise 2.1 — Polynomials

NCERT Class 10 Maths, Chapter 2

Key Concepts

The zeroes of a polynomial p(x) are the x-coordinates of the points where the graph of y = p(x) crosses (or touches) the x-axis. A polynomial of degree n has at most n zeroes.

Solutions

Q1. The graphs of y = p(x) are given in Fig. 2.10 below, for some polynomials p(x). Find the number of zeroes of p(x), in each case.
Why this answer: count how many times each curve crosses (or touches) the x-axis — that count is the number of zeroes.
(i) The curve does not touch the x-axis at all.
(ii) The curve crosses the x-axis at exactly 1 point.
(iii) The curve crosses the x-axis at 3 points.
(iv) The curve crosses the x-axis at 2 points.
(v) The curve crosses the x-axis at 4 points.
(vi) The curve crosses the x-axis at 3 points.
Answer: (i) 0 zeroes   (ii) 1 zero   (iii) 3 zeroes   (iv) 2 zeroes   (v) 4 zeroes   (vi) 3 zeroes
Common mistake: counting a point where the curve just touches the x-axis (without crossing to the other side) as "not a zero" — a touch point still counts as one zero, since p(x) = 0 there.

Key Points

  • Number of zeroes of p(x) = number of points where the graph of y = p(x) meets the x-axis (crossing or touching).
  • A polynomial of degree n has at most n zeroes — this graph-reading skill is the geometric way to see that rule in action.
