About Chapter 4 – Quadratic Equations

Chapter 4 of Class 10 Maths is Quadratic Equations. In this chapter you will learn:

  • Standard form of a quadratic equation: ax² + bx + c = 0
  • Solving by factorisation method
  • Solving using the quadratic formula
  • Nature of roots using discriminant (D = b² – 4ac)
  • Real life word problems based on quadratic equations

Key Formulas – Chapter 4 Quadratic Equations

  • Standard form: ax² + bx + c = 0, where a ≠ 0
  • Quadratic Formula: x = (–b ± √(b²–4ac)) / 2a
  • Discriminant: D = b² – 4ac
  • D > 0 → Two distinct real roots
  • D = 0 → Two equal real roots
  • D < 0 → No real roots
  • Sum of roots: α + β = –b/a
  • Product of roots: αβ = c/a

Important Points to Remember

  • A quadratic equation has degree 2.
  • It can have at most 2 roots.
  • Always arrange in standard form ax² + bx + c = 0 before solving.
  • Factorisation works when roots are rational numbers.
  • Use quadratic formula when factorisation is difficult.
  • Check discriminant first to know how many roots exist.
