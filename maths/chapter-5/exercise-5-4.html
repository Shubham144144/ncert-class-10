Key Formulas Used:

  • nth term: aₙ = a + (n–1)d
  • Sum of n terms: Sₙ = n⁄2 [2a + (n–1)d]
  • Sum using last term: Sₙ = n⁄2 (a + l)

Question 1

Which term of the AP: 121, 117, 113, ... is its first negative term?

Solution:

a = 121, d = 117 – 121 = –4

For first negative term: aₙ < 0

a + (n–1)d < 0

121 + (n–1)(–4) < 0

121 – 4n + 4 < 0

125 – 4n < 0

4n > 125

n > 125⁄4 = 31.25

So smallest whole number value of n = 32

Answer: 32nd term is the first negative term.

Question 2

The sum of the third and the seventh terms of an AP is 6 and their product is 8. Find the sum of first sixteen terms of the AP.

Solution:

Step 1: Let a₃ = a + 2d and a₇ = a + 6d

a₃ + a₇ = 6 → (a + 2d) + (a + 6d) = 6 → 2a + 8d = 6 → a + 4d = 3 ... (1)

a₃ × a₇ = 8 → (a + 2d)(a + 6d) = 8 ... (2)

Step 2: From (1): a = 3 – 4d

Substitute in (2):

(3 – 4d + 2d)(3 – 4d + 6d) = 8

(3 – 2d)(3 + 2d) = 8

9 – 4d² = 8

4d² = 1

d² = 1⁄4

d = 1⁄2 or d = –1⁄2

Step 3: When d = 1⁄2: a = 3 – 4(1⁄2) = 3 – 2 = 1

When d = –1⁄2: a = 3 – 4(–1⁄2) = 3 + 2 = 5

Step 4: Find S₁₆ in both cases.

Case 1: a = 1, d = 1⁄2

S₁₆ = 16⁄2[2(1) + 15(1⁄2)] = 8[2 + 15⁄2] = 8 × 19⁄2 = 76

Case 2: a = 5, d = –1⁄2

S₁₆ = 16⁄2[2(5) + 15(–1⁄2)] = 8[10 – 15⁄2] = 8 × 5⁄2 = 20

Answer: Sum of first 16 terms = 76 or 20.

Question 3

A ladder has rungs 25 cm apart. The rungs decrease uniformly in length from 45 cm at the bottom to 25 cm at the top. If the top and the bottom rungs are 21⁄2 m apart, what is the length of the wood required for the rungs?

Solution:

Step 1: Distance between top and bottom = 21⁄2 m = 250 cm

Number of rungs = 250⁄25 + 1 = 10 + 1 = 11

Step 2: Lengths of rungs form an AP.

a = 45 cm (bottom rung), l = 25 cm (top rung), n = 11

Step 3: Total length = S₁₁ = 11⁄2(45 + 25) = 11⁄2 × 70 = 385 cm

Answer: Total length of wood required = 385 cm.

Question 4

The houses of a row are numbered consecutively from 1 to 49. Show that there is a value of x such that the sum of the numbers of the houses preceding the house numbered x is equal to the sum of the numbers of the houses following it. Find this value of x.

Solution:

Step 1: Sum of house numbers before x = S(x–1) = (x–1)x⁄2

Step 2: Sum of house numbers after x = S₄₉ – Sx

S₄₉ = 49 × 50⁄2 = 1225

Sx = x(x+1)⁄2

Sum after x = 1225 – x(x+1)⁄2

Step 3: Set sum before x = sum after x

(x–1)x⁄2 = 1225 – x(x+1)⁄2

x(x–1)⁄2 + x(x+1)⁄2 = 1225

x⁄2[(x–1) + (x+1)] = 1225

x⁄2 × 2x = 1225

x² = 1225

x = 35

Verify:

Sum before 35 = 34 × 35⁄2 = 595

Sum after 35 = 1225 – 35 × 36⁄2 = 1225 – 630 = 595 ✅

Answer: x = 35

Question 5

A small terrace at a football ground comprises of 15 steps each of which is 50 m long and built of solid concrete. Each step has a rise of 1⁄4 m and a tread of 1⁄2 m. Calculate the total volume of concrete required to build the terrace.

Solution:

Step 1: Volume of each step.

Step 1 (bottom): volume = 1⁄4 × 1⁄2 × 50 = 25⁄4 m³

Step 2: volume = 2 × 1⁄4 × 1⁄2 × 50 = 50⁄4 m³

Step 3: volume = 3 × 1⁄4 × 1⁄2 × 50 = 75⁄4 m³

Volumes form an AP: 25⁄4, 50⁄4, 75⁄4, ...

Step 2: Total volume = S₁₅

a = 25⁄4, d = 25⁄4, n = 15

S₁₅ = 15⁄2[2 × 25⁄4 + 14 × 25⁄4]

= 15⁄2 × 25⁄4 × 16

= 15⁄2 × 100

= 750

Answer: Total volume of concrete = 750 m³

Key Points – Exercise 5.4

  • For first negative term: set aₙ < 0 and find smallest whole number n.
  • Use algebraic identities like (a+b)(a–b) = a²–b² to simplify.
  • Always verify answers by substituting back.
  • Real life problems: identify a, d and n carefully from given conditions.
