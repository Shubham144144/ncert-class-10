Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic: Every composite number can be expressed as a product of prime numbers in a unique way (order does not matter).

Question 1

Question:

Express each number as a product of its prime factors:

(i) 140   (ii) 156   (iii) 3825   (iv) 5005   (v) 7429

Solution (i): 140

Step 1: Divide by smallest prime 2.

140 ÷ 2 = 70

Step 2: 70 ÷ 2 = 35

Step 3: 35 not divisible by 2. Try 5.

35 ÷ 5 = 7

Step 4: 7 is prime. Stop.

Answer: 140 = 2² × 5 × 7

Solution (ii): 156

Step 1: 156 ÷ 2 = 78

Step 2: 78 ÷ 2 = 39

Step 3: 39 ÷ 3 = 13

Step 4: 13 is prime. Stop.

Answer: 156 = 2² × 3 × 13

Solution (iii): 3825

Step 1: 3825 is odd. Try 3.

3825 ÷ 3 = 1275

Step 2: 1275 ÷ 3 = 425

Step 3: 425 ÷ 5 = 85

Step 4: 85 ÷ 5 = 17

Step 5: 17 is prime. Stop.

Answer: 3825 = 3² × 5² × 17

Solution (iv): 5005

Step 1: 5005 ÷ 5 = 1001

Step 2: 1001 ÷ 7 = 143

Step 3: 143 ÷ 11 = 13

Step 4: 13 is prime. Stop.

Answer: 5005 = 5 × 7 × 11 × 13

Solution (v): 7429

Step 1: Not divisible by 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13. Try 17.

7429 ÷ 17 = 437

Step 2: 437 ÷ 19 = 23

Step 3: 23 is prime. Stop.

Answer: 7429 = 17 × 19 × 23

Question 2

Question:

Find the LCM and HCF of the following pairs of integers and verify that LCM × HCF = product of the two numbers:

(i) 26 and 91   (ii) 510 and 92   (iii) 336 and 54

Solution (i): 26 and 91

Step 1: Prime factorisation.

26 = 2 × 13

91 = 7 × 13

Step 2: HCF = common factor with lowest power = 13

Step 3: LCM = all factors with highest power = 2 × 7 × 13 = 182

Step 4: Verify: LCM × HCF = 182 × 13 = 2366

26 × 91 = 2366 ✅

Answer: HCF = 13, LCM = 182

Solution (ii): 510 and 92

Step 1: Prime factorisation.

510 = 2 × 3 × 5 × 17

92 = 2² × 23

Step 2: HCF = 2

Step 3: LCM = 2² × 3 × 5 × 17 × 23 = 23460

Step 4: Verify: 23460 × 2 = 46920

510 × 92 = 46920 ✅

Answer: HCF = 2, LCM = 23460

Solution (iii): 336 and 54

Step 1: Prime factorisation.

336 = 2⁴ × 3 × 7

54 = 2 × 3³

Step 2: HCF = 2¹ × 3¹ = 6

Step 3: LCM = 2⁴ × 3³ × 7 = 16 × 27 × 7 = 3024

Step 4: Verify: 3024 × 6 = 18144

336 × 54 = 18144 ✅

Answer: HCF = 6, LCM = 3024

Question 3

Question:

Find the LCM and HCF of the following integers by applying the prime factorisation method:

(i) 12, 15 and 21   (ii) 17, 23 and 29   (iii) 8, 9 and 25

Solution (i): 12, 15 and 21

12 = 2² × 3   15 = 3 × 5   21 = 3 × 7

HCF = 3 (only common factor)

LCM = 2² × 3 × 5 × 7 = 420

Answer: HCF = 3, LCM = 420

Solution (ii): 17, 23 and 29

All three are prime numbers. No common factor.

HCF = 1

LCM = 17 × 23 × 29 = 11339

Answer: HCF = 1, LCM = 11339

Solution (iii): 8, 9 and 25

8 = 2³   9 = 3²   25 = 5²

No common factor among all three.

HCF = 1

LCM = 2³ × 3² × 5² = 1800

Answer: HCF = 1, LCM = 1800

Question 4

Question:

Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, find LCM (306, 657).

Solution:

Step 1: Formula: LCM × HCF = Product of two numbers

Step 2: LCM × 9 = 306 × 657 = 201042

Step 3: LCM = 201042 ÷ 9 = 22338

Answer: LCM (306, 657) = 22338

Question 5

Question:

Check whether 6ⁿ can end with the digit 0 for any natural number n.

Solution:

Step 1: For a number to end with 0, it must have both 2 and 5 as prime factors (since 10 = 2 × 5).

Step 2: Prime factorisation of 6ⁿ:

6 = 2 × 3, so 6ⁿ = 2ⁿ × 3ⁿ

Step 3: 6ⁿ only has 2 and 3 as prime factors. It does NOT have 5.

Step 4: Since 5 is not a factor, 6ⁿ cannot be divisible by 10.

Answer: 6ⁿ can NEVER end with digit 0 for any natural number n.

Question 6

Question:

Explain why 7 × 11 × 13 + 13 and 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5 are composite numbers.

Solution:

Part 1: 7 × 11 × 13 + 13

Step 1: Take 13 common.

= 13 × (7 × 11 + 1) = 13 × (77 + 1) = 13 × 78

Step 2: It has factors 13 and 78. So it is composite. ✅

Part 2: 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5

Step 1: Take 5 common.

= 5 × (7 × 6 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 1) = 5 × (1008 + 1) = 5 × 1009

Step 2: It has factors 5 and 1009. So it is composite. ✅

Answer: Both are composite numbers because they have factors other than 1 and themselves.

Question 7

Question:

There is a circular path around a sports field. Sonia takes 18 minutes to drive one round of the field, while Ravi takes 12 minutes for the same. Suppose they both start at the same point and at the same time, and go in the same direction. After how many minutes will they meet again at the starting point?

Solution:

Step 1: They will meet after LCM of 18 and 12 minutes.

Step 2: Prime factorisation:

18 = 2 × 3²   12 = 2² × 3

Step 3: LCM = 2² × 3² = 4 × 9 = 36

Answer: Sonia and Ravi will meet again after 36 minutes.

Key Points – Exercise 1.1

  • Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic: Every composite number = unique product of primes.
  • HCF: Common prime factors with lowest power.
  • LCM: All prime factors with highest power.
  • Formula: HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers.
  • If HCF = 1, numbers are co-prime.
