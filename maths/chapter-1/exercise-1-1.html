Exercise 1.1 — Real Numbers

NCERT Class 10 Maths, Chapter 1

Key Concepts

This exercise uses the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic (every composite number is a unique product of primes) to find prime factorisations, and to compute HCF and LCM using the prime factorisation method.

Solutions

Q1. Express each number as a product of its prime factors:
Why this answer: keep dividing by the smallest prime until only 1 remains.
(i) 140 = 2 × 2 × 5 × 7
Answer: 140 = 22 × 5 × 7
(ii) 156 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 13
Answer: 156 = 22 × 3 × 13
(iii) 3825 = 3 × 3 × 5 × 5 × 17
Answer: 3825 = 32 × 52 × 17
(iv) 5005 = 5 × 7 × 11 × 13
Answer: 5005 = 5 × 7 × 11 × 13
(v) 7429 = 17 × 19 × 23
Answer: 7429 = 17 × 19 × 23
Common mistake: students often stop testing primes too early and miss a larger prime factor like 23 — always divide until the quotient itself is prime.
Q2. Find the LCM and HCF of the following pairs of integers and verify that LCM × HCF = product of the two numbers.
Why this answer: HCF uses the lowest common powers, LCM uses the highest powers, of all prime factors involved.
(i) 26 = 2 × 13, 91 = 7 × 13
HCF = 13, LCM = 2 × 7 × 13 = 182
Check: LCM × HCF = 182 × 13 = 2366 = 26 × 91 ✓
Answer: HCF = 13, LCM = 182
(ii) 510 = 2 × 3 × 5 × 17, 92 = 22 × 23
HCF = 2, LCM = 22 × 3 × 5 × 17 × 23 = 23460
Check: LCM × HCF = 23460 × 2 = 46920 = 510 × 92 ✓
Answer: HCF = 2, LCM = 23460
(iii) 336 = 24 × 3 × 7, 54 = 2 × 33
HCF = 2 × 3 = 6, LCM = 24 × 33 × 7 = 3024
Check: LCM × HCF = 3024 × 6 = 18144 = 336 × 54 ✓
Answer: HCF = 6, LCM = 3024
Q3. Find the LCM and HCF of the following integers by applying the prime factorisation method.
(i) 12 = 22 × 3, 15 = 3 × 5, 21 = 3 × 7
HCF = 3, LCM = 22 × 3 × 5 × 7 = 420
Answer: HCF = 3, LCM = 420
(ii) 17, 23, 29 are all prime numbers with no common factors.
HCF = 1, LCM = 17 × 23 × 29 = 11339
Answer: HCF = 1, LCM = 11339
(iii) 8 = 23, 9 = 32, 25 = 52 share no common prime factor.
HCF = 1, LCM = 23 × 32 × 52 = 1800
Answer: HCF = 1, LCM = 1800
Q4. Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, find LCM (306, 657).
Why this answer: for any two numbers, HCF × LCM = product of the numbers.
LCM = 306 × 6579 = 2010429 = 22338
Answer: LCM (306, 657) = 22338
Q5. Check whether 6n can end with the digit 0 for any natural number n.
Why this answer: a number ends in 0 only if it is divisible by 10 = 2 × 5, so it must contain 5 as a prime factor.
6n = (2 × 3)n = 2n × 3n.
By the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, the prime factorisation of any number is unique — so the only prime factors of 6n are 2 and 3. Since 5 never appears, 6n is never divisible by 10.
Answer: No, 6n can never end with the digit 0 for any natural number n.
Common mistake: writing "6¹ = 6, 6² = 36..." and guessing from a pattern instead of proving it using prime factorisation — CBSE marking requires the prime-factorisation argument.
Q6. Explain why 7 × 11 × 13 + 13 and 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5 are composite numbers.
Why this answer: if an expression can be factored into two numbers, each greater than 1, it is composite by definition.
7 × 11 × 13 + 13 = 13 × (7 × 11 + 1) = 13 × 78 = 1014, which is divisible by 13 and 78, so it is composite.
7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5 = 5 × (7 × 6 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 1) = 5 × 1009 = 5045, which is divisible by 5 and 1009, so it is composite.
Answer: Both numbers are composite, since each factors as a product of two integers greater than 1.
Q7. There is a circular path around a sports field. Sonia takes 18 minutes to drive one round of the field, while Ravi takes 12 minutes for the same. Suppose they both start at the same point and at the same time, and go in the same direction. After how many minutes will they meet again at the starting point?
Why this answer: they'll both be back at the starting point at any common multiple of their individual times — the first such time is the LCM.
18 = 2 × 32, 12 = 22 × 3
LCM (18, 12) = 22 × 32 = 36
Answer: They will meet again at the starting point after 36 minutes.

Key Points

  • The Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic: every composite number can be factorised as a product of primes, uniquely (apart from the order of factors).
  • HCF = product of the smallest powers of common prime factors; LCM = product of the greatest powers of all prime factors involved.
  • For any two positive integers a and b: HCF(a, b) × LCM(a, b) = a × b. This shortcut does not extend directly to three or more numbers.
  • "After how many minutes will they meet again" type problems are solved using LCM.
